# ADA Title II Final Rule — Digital Accessibility

## Overview

On April 24, 2024, the U.S. Department of Justice published a final rule under Title II of the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) establishing specific requirements for state and local government entities to make their web content and mobile applications accessible to people with disabilities.

The rule adopts WCAG 2.1 Level AA as the technical standard that covered entities must follow.

## Who Must Comply

Title II of the ADA applies to **state and local government entities**, which includes:
- State governments and agencies
- County and city governments
- School districts
- Public universities and colleges
- Transportation authorities
- Public libraries
- Courts and judicial systems
- Law enforcement agencies
- Public housing authorities
- Any program or service receiving government funding

## What Must Be Accessible

The rule covers:
- **Web content**: Websites, web applications, and web-based services
- **Mobile applications**: Native and hybrid mobile apps
- **Content provided through contractors**: If a government entity provides content through a vendor, that content must also be accessible
- **Online forms and services**: Applications, registrations, payments
- **Documents published online**: PDFs, Word documents, spreadsheets posted on government websites (with some exceptions)

## Compliance Timelines

The rule establishes a phased timeline based on population size:

| Entity Type | Compliance Deadline |
|-------------|-------------------|
| Entities with populations of 50,000 or more | **April 24, 2026** |
| Entities with populations under 50,000 | **April 26, 2027** |
| Special districts (regardless of population) | **April 26, 2028** |

## Technical Standard

The required technical standard is **WCAG 2.1 Level AA**, which includes:
- All Level A success criteria (30 criteria)
- All Level AA success criteria (20 criteria)
- Does NOT require Level AAA criteria

## Enforcement

The DOJ can enforce compliance through:
- Investigation of complaints
- Compliance reviews
- Litigation
- Settlement agreements

Individuals can also file private lawsuits under Title II.
