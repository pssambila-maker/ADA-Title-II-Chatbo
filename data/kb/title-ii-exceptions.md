# Title II Content Exceptions

## Overview

The DOJ Title II rule includes limited exceptions for certain categories of web content. However, even when a technical exception applies, other Title II obligations such as effective communication requirements may still require alternative accessible formats.

## Exception Categories

### 1. Archived Web Content

Web content qualifies as "archived" if it meets ALL of the following criteria:
- Created before the compliance date
- Maintained exclusively for reference, research, or recordkeeping
- NOT altered or updated after the compliance date
- Organized and stored in a dedicated area or areas clearly identified as archived content

**Important**: Content that is actively used for current programs, services, or activities is NOT archived, even if labeled as such.

### 2. Preexisting Conventional Electronic Documents

Documents such as PDFs, Word documents, and spreadsheets that were published before the compliance date may be excepted IF:
- They were posted before the entity's compliance date
- They are not currently used to apply for, gain access to, or participate in a program or service

**Key limitation**: If a user requests an accessible version of an excepted document as a reasonable modification, the entity must still provide it under general Title II obligations.

### 3. Third-Party Content

Content posted by a third party on a government entity's website may be excepted IF:
- The entity did not create or procure the content
- The entity does not exercise editorial control over the content
- Examples: public comments on a government social media page, user-submitted content

**Limitation**: This does NOT apply to content provided by vendors or contractors under agreement with the government entity.

### 4. Individualized, Password-Protected Documents

Documents that are:
- Tailored for a specific individual
- Available only via a password-protected or otherwise secured portal
- Examples: individual tax statements, personalized benefit letters

These may have alternative compliance paths.

### 5. Preexisting Social Media Posts

Social media content posted before the compliance date is generally excepted, provided:
- It was posted before the entity's compliance deadline
- New posts made after the deadline must be accessible

## When Exceptions Do NOT Apply

Even when a technical exception exists:
- **Effective communication**: Title II still requires entities to provide effective communication, which may mean providing accessible alternatives upon request
- **Reasonable modifications**: Entities must make reasonable modifications to policies to ensure equal access
- **Newly posted content**: All content posted after the compliance date must meet WCAG 2.1 Level AA
